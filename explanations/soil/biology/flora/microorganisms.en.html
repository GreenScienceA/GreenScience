Fungal hyphae physically enmesh soil particles, creating stable macroaggregates (>250 Âµm). Fungi vs. Bacteria: The F:B Ratio

The fungal-to-bacterial (F:B) biomass ratio is a key indicator of soil health and management intensity. Undisturbed ecosystems (forests, grasslands) tend to be fungi-dominated, while heavily tilled agricultural soils shift toward bacteria-dominated communities. This ratio reflects fundamental differences in decomposition pathways: the "fast" bacterial channel processes labile carbon quickly, while the "slow" fungal channel processes recalcitrant carbon and stores it more efficiently in the soil (Six et al., 2006).
