In the arid and semi-arid zones of North Africa, the Middle East, and the Sahel â€” regions that include much of Algeria's agricultural land â€” soil amendments take on special significance. The dominant challenges are salinity, sodicity, alkalinity, and low organic matter, all intensified by high evapotranspiration, limited rainfall, and irrigation with saline or sodic water.

Biochar, produced from agricultural waste (olive pomace, date palm fronds, cereal straw), represents an emerging opportunity for simultaneous soil improvement and carbon sequestration in these challenging environments, where soil organic matter is naturally low and difficult to maintain (Lehmann & Joseph, 2015).
